About UTCH

The UT Canoe and Hiking Club is the oldest and largest outdoors club on campus, student run and led since 1968.

FAQS

How do I become a member?

UTCH recruits new members year-round; in order to go on trips you need to join "Canoe and Hiking Club" on Vollink, fill out the waiver, and pay dues ($50/year or $35/semester) in cash or to @UT-Canoe-Hiking on Venmo.

What is included after I pay the dues?

Once you pay dues, you are allowed to sign up for trips and use our equipment. We have the necessary gear for all of our trips, including canoes, rafts, backpacks, tents, sleeping gear, and headlamps. All you need to bring is clothes and food!

Is this club beginner friendly?

Yes! We welcome people of all skill levels; because we have all of the gear that you need, we offer the cheapest way to go on new adventures and spark your love for the outdoors.

What if I don't have a car?

You need not worry, we carpool from campus for each adventure that we do.

Where does UTCH go?

We do trips all over Tennessee and the United States. There are many local backpacking/hiking trips (Carvers Gap, Smokey Mountains, Grayson Highlands, Big South Fork), canoeing/rafting trips (Ocoee, Toccoa, Clear Creek), along with two larger trips over spring and winter break (Cumberland Island, Big Bend, Gila, Superstitions)
